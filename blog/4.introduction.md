Congratulations on getting into FTU CS! This guide should help you, if you're a beginner, to easily start and ease the next 4 years at the university.

## 0. Prequisites: Laptops and operating systems

### Laptops

While we provide you with pretty powerful machines at B410 and B411, a laptop is generally recommended in order for you to continue learning and do your own work anywhere.

It's up to your economical status and your preferences to choose the right machine. As cloud services become more and more accessible, you won't need a "chonky" machine to get things done. For example, with Google Colab, you can have access to NVIDIA L4 GPUs - that's a better GPU to train AI models than a RTX 4060 Laptop - all at no cost*.

> *_There is a limit of 24 hours of maximum runtime for each session on Google Colab. Check out your desired service provider documentation for more details._

### Operating systems

**It is highly recommended that you use Linux** for any coding-related tasks.

Linux, being part of the `*nix` family, is the **[kernel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kernel_(operating_system))** of which Linux-based operating systems are built upon. Linux is present from the most powerful supercomputers, to all Android phones, even your fridge, and the Ingenuity Mars Helicopter that isn't on our planet.

> _In this guide and throughout most of other guides written (unless stated otherwise), the author assumes you have at least basic knowledge of `*nix` commands._

You won't have to give up Windows, macOS, or any other opearting systems. **Windows supports [Windows Subsystem for Linux (WSL)](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/wsl/install), and macOS is actually a far cousin of Linux of the `*nix` family** - that is, there is a good amount of commands and syntaxs that are similar to Linux.

## 1. Basic definitions and commands

## Definitions

> These definitions are abstract and not official.

* Directory: equals "folder" - a place including your files
* CLI: Command Line Interface: where you type your command.
* Terminal: creates a CLI.
* IDE: Integrated Development Enviroment: where you write your code (e.g. Visual Studio Code, CodeBlocks, vim, nano, ...)
* Shell: An application to help you type your commands with features such as auto-complete, etc... (e.g. `bash`, `zsh`, `fish`, ...)
* Distro: Linux Distribution: a full operating system, with Linux as its kernel (e.g. Ubuntu, Fedora, Arch Linux, ...)


## Commands

* `ls`: Lists all files in the current directory.
* `cd <path/to/directory>`: **c**hanges the **d**irectory to the desired one.
* `pwd`: Prints the full path to current working directory.
  > On Ubuntu, the default behaviour of the default shell (`bash`) should print your current working directory by default. Other shells/distros may have different behaviours.
